Lights Out Christmas

CORE GAMEPLAY
The side-scrolling screen moves at a constant pace like Paperboy, set against a dark winter night sky. The squirrel moves across multiple "lanes" — ground level, fence tops, tree branches, power lines, and rooftops — each with different speeds, hazards, and access to different light strings. Biting through a light string triggers a satisfying cascade effect where the whole strand goes dark. Bonus points for taking out an entire house's display in one run.
________________________________________
SCORING
•	Lights bitten = base points
•	Full house blackout = big multiplier
•	Chain reactions (one cut kills multiple strands) = combo bonus
•	Speed bonus for doing it quickly before the homeowner notices
•	Neighborhood darkness meter that fills as you wreak havoc — fill it completely for a stage bonus
________________________________________
POWER UPS
•	Acorn Stash — temporary speed boost, the squirrel is caffeinated and frantic
•	Winter Coat — brief invincibility, homeowners can't grab you
•	Shadow Mode — the squirrel goes nearly invisible, won't trigger porch lights or homeowner alerts
•	Super Chomp — bite through reinforced or tangled light strings instantly
•	Decoy Nut — toss an acorn to distract a chasing homeowner or a dog
•	Ice Patch — freeze a pursuing threat in place temporarily
•	Double Tail — splits into two squirrels briefly, covering twice the ground
•	Frenzy Mode — everything slows down slightly while you move at full speed (bullet-time style)
________________________________________
THREATS
•	Homeowners — alerted when you're caught in the light of an active display; they run out with varying speeds (the grumpy old man is slow but relentless, the dad in pajamas is fast but gives up easily)
•	Porch Lights — motion-activated, if they catch you they alert the homeowner
•	Dogs — chained at first but can reach certain ground-level paths, bark to alert owners even if they can't reach you
•	Cats — sneaky, don't alert anyone but will chase you across rooftops and power lines
•	Ice patches on roofs — slippery, hard to control
•	Tangled lights — some strands are knotted and take multiple bites, slowing you down dangerously
•	Ladder-climbing homeowners — if you've been spotted, some homeowners will anticipate your rooftop path and try to cut you off
•	Neighborhood Watch car — slowly cruises the street; if you're on the ground when it passes, it's game over
•	Owl — lurks in trees at night, grabs you if you stay on a branch too long
________________________________________
LEVEL / WORLD IDEAS
•	Suburban Street — starter level, simple house layouts, basic displays
•	Rich Neighborhood — elaborate synchronized displays, harder to fully black out but massive points
•	The Cul-de-Sac — the screen loops slightly, disorienting, homeowners coordinate
•	Christmas Eve — everyone is home and hyper-vigilant, maximum threat density
•	The Town Square — a massive community display with a giant Christmas tree at the center as the ultimate target
________________________________________
VISUAL / AESTHETIC IDEAS
•	Deep blue-black night sky with pixel stars and a full moon
•	Warm glowing light from windows contrasting against the cold dark street
•	When a strand goes dark, it flickers and pops with a little pixel spark effect
•	The squirrel has a little mischievous animation when it bites through a strand
•	Snow falling in the background, accumulating on rooftops
•	Each house has a distinct personality visible through pixel art — inflatable decorations, lawn nativity scenes, candy cane path lights, roof Santas
•	Score combos flash in big retro arcade font
________________________________________
AUDIO IDEAS
•	Chiptune remix of Christmas carols that gets more frantic as the chaos meter rises
•	Satisfying "pop and fizzle" sound when lights go out
•	Homeowner shout samples in 8-bit style ("Hey! Get outta there!")
•	The neighborhood gets progressively quieter and darker as you succeed — a creeping silence that feels rewarding
________________________________________
PROGRESSION IDEAS
•	Upgrade your squirrel between levels — faster bite, quieter movement, longer jump
•	Unlock new squirrel skins (e.g. tiny Santa hat, elf costume worn ironically)
•	Leaderboard for total neighborhood darkness percentage
•	A final boss level where you face the most elaborately decorated house on the block, guarded by the most alert homeowner and a particularly aggressive dog
